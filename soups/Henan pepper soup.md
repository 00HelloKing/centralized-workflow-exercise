# Henan pepper Soup

## Introduction
*A sour and spicy soup with intense pepper aroma, the ultimate comforting breakfast for Henan locals!*

---

## Ingredients (4 servings)
### Main Ingredients
• **100g** beef or lamb, thinly sliced or diced, traditionally uses cooked beef  
• **50g** black fungus, soaked and shredded  
• **50g** daylily flowers, soaked and cut into sections  
• **100g** wheat gluten or tofu skin strips  
• **1 piece** firm tofu, cubed  
• **1 piece** vermicelli, soaked and cut short  

### Soup Base Seasonings
• **2 tbsp** sweet potato starch for thickening  
• **1.5L** beef bone broth or water, broth preferred  
• **3 tbsp** aged vinegar for sourness  
• **1 tbsp** light soy sauce  
• **1 tsp** dark soy sauce for color  

### Spice Mix (key!)
• **2 tsp** white pepper powder, the soul of this dish!  
• **1 tsp** Sichuan pepper powder  
• **½ tsp** Chinese 13-spice  
• **½ tsp** chicken powder, optional  

### Garnishes
• **1 tbsp** minced ginger  
• chopped scallions to taste  
• **1 tbsp** sesame oil  
• salt to taste  

---

## Instructions

### 1️⃣ Prep Ingredients
• Marinate beef slices with cooking wine and starch for 10 minutes. Skip if using cooked beef.  
• Soak fungus, daylily, and vermicelli in warm water. Blanch tofu to remove beany taste.  

### 2️⃣ Sauté Base
• Stir-fry ginger in oil, then add beef until color changes. Briefly fry if using cooked beef.  
• Add daylily and fungus, stir-fry 1 minute.  

### 3️⃣ Simmer Soup
• Add broth, boil then simmer for 10 minutes.  
• Add tofu, gluten, and vermicelli, cook 5 more minutes.  

### 4️⃣ Season and Thicken
• Add soy sauces and salt. Add pepper powders in two batches - half while cooking, half before serving.  
• Mix starch with water, stir into soup until desired thickness (like thin yogurt).  

### 5️⃣ Finishing Touches
• Turn off heat, add vinegar (to preserve sourness), 13-spice, chicken powder, and sesame oil.  

### 6️⃣ Serve
• Ladle into bowls, garnish with scallions. Serve with youtiao, pan-fried buns or sesame cakes. Enjoy hot!

---

## Tips
1. **Pepper is key**: Authentic spiciness comes from white pepper - don't skimp!
2. **Balance sour & spicy**: Add vinegar last to prevent bitterness.
3. **Vegetarian option**: Replace meat with mushrooms and veggie broth.
